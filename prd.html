PRD: phuonganh.pm - Personal Portfolio Site
PA
Owned by Le Thi Phuong Anh · edited just now, allegedly ✓ Shipped
📄 Export ⭐ Watching 💬 4 comments

Yes, this is a real PRD, about a portfolio site, written by the person the portfolio is for. No, I will not be taking questions about what this says about me.

1. Problem Statement

Job-seeking PMs in the tech market mostly look identical on paper: a PDF resume, a LinkedIn profile, maybe a Notion page with bullet points copied from the CV. None of that gives a hiring manager a reason to remember a candidate specifically, and none of it demonstrates product thinking - it just describes it.

I needed something that did three jobs at once:

  1. Pass the 10-second scan test (what do you do, what have you shipped, can I contact you)
  2. Prove product judgment through the form of the artifact, not just its content
  3. Be memorable enough that a recruiter looking at portfolio #38 that day actually remembers mine

2. Goals

  • Primary: Get more interview callbacks per application sent, specifically for PM roles at consumer product companies
  • Secondary: Signal a transferable skill set if I eventually pivot toward game industry roles
  • Tertiary: Have something I'm not embarrassed to put on a resume, unlike a generic Notion doc

3. Non-Goals

  • Not trying to win a design award - visual polish is in service of memorability, not the goal itself
  • Not building a CMS or anything dynamic - static site, manually edited, that's fine at this scale
  • Not optimizing for every possible employer - explicitly accepting tone risk with more conservative reviewers in exchange for higher signal with the right ones (see Risks)

4. Target User

Primary: A hiring manager or recruiter at a consumer-facing tech company, mid-funnel in their hiring process, who has 5–10 portfolios to review in one sitting and has already skimmed a hundred LinkedIn profiles that all sound the same.

User need: Decide in under 2 minutes whether this candidate is worth a 30-minute screening call.

ℹ️ Explicitly not optimized for: highly conservative or traditional enterprise reviewers who read playful design as a lack of seriousness. Known tradeoff, not an oversight - a more conservative fallback variant exists for specific applications where this risk matters more.

5. Solution

A single-page site styled as a low-fidelity wireframe / Balsamiq mockup - using the medium itself as the argument: a PM who thinks in flows, tickets, and structured documents, presented through an artifact that looks exactly like the tools she uses daily.

SectionJob to be done
HeroState the thesis in one line: finds the real problem, builds the fix
Career FlowShow the throughline across 3 job titles as one consistent skill, not a scattered job history
Selected Work (tickets)3 real case studies - problem → approach → impact - with live links to the shipped products
Side QuestHonest signal of a personal interest (game design) without overclaiming expertise
360° ReviewAnonymous comedic "testimonials" surfacing real behavioral traits without sounding self-written
Skills BoardCategorized competencies with honest self-assessment tiers
ContactSelf-selecting fit criteria + two CTAs (email, CV download)
This PageReward someone for actually digging around instead of skimming. You. Specifically you.

6. User Stories & Acceptance Criteria

Yes, I wrote real Gherkin-style user stories for my own portfolio. No, I don't think that's normal. I did it anyway.

US-01 · P0
As a hiring manager with 40 tabs open and the will to live slowly draining, I want to understand what this person actually does within 10 seconds, so that I don't have to read an entire case study just to decide if I should keep reading.
  • ✓ Hero states the thesis in one sentence, no scrolling required
  • ✓ Career flow shows 3 job titles resolving into one throughline, not three unrelated vibes
  • ✓ Given I only read the hero and bounce, I should still know what role to email her about
  • ✓ Given I am extremely tired, the page should not ask anything of me beyond "scroll"
US-02 · P0
As someone professionally trained to be suspicious of resume bullet points, I want to verify the case studies are real and not embellished, so that I don't get blindsided in the interview when I ask a follow-up.
  • ✓ Every case study links to the live, real product
  • ✓ Stats are specific enough to be falsifiable, not vague enough to dodge a follow-up question
  • ✓ Given a skeptical reader, the links should survive a 2-second sanity check and ideally a 10-minute one too
US-03 · P1
As a person whose entire job is downloading PDFs named "Final_v2_FINAL", I want the "Download CV" button to behave like a normal button, so that I don't have to email her asking where the actual file went.
  • ✓ Clicking "Download CV" downloads an actual PDF, not a 404 with a side of embarrassment
  • ✓ Filename matches between the HTML and uploaded file (see: Risks, "Fragility" - yes, I broke this myself, no I will not be elaborating further)
  • ✓ Given the file is hosted next to the page, it should not silently die the moment the repo gets reorganized at 1am
US-04 · P2, but emotionally P0
As the one person who actually reads the footer instead of immediately closing the tab, I want to be rewarded for paying attention, so that snooping around someone's portfolio at 11pm feels like time well spent.
  • ✓ A genuinely hidden link exists, disguised as normal, boring footer text
  • ✓ A console message exists for the specific subset of people who open dev tools for no reason, like feral raccoons
  • ✓ Given I find either path, I should land on a document that rewards the effort instead of a 404 that makes me feel stupid for trying
US-05 · Will not fix
As me, at 1am, writing acceptance criteria for my own personal website, I want to acknowledge that this is a lot of process for a portfolio, so that future-me reading this doesn't think past-me has completely lost the plot.
  • ✓ This story exists
  • ✓ I have made peace with it
  • ✓ Given that you're reading this far, you have too

7. Success Metrics

Since this isn't an A/B-testable product, the real success metrics are qualitative and slow-feedback:

  • Interview callback rate per application where this link was included vs. a plain CV-only application
  • Anecdotal: does a recruiter mention the portfolio unprompted in a screening call
  • Whether anyone actually clicks through to onehub.vn / herond.org / nobinobi.vn (can't currently instrument this - see Open Questions)
  • Whether anyone finds this exact page. If you're reading this, that metric just incremented by one.

8. Risks & Mitigations

RiskMitigation
Tone risk Humor reads as "not serious enough" for conservative employers Accepted A neutral fallback variant exists for applications where this risk is too high
Visual risk The wireframe aesthetic itself reads as unconventional, independent of jokes Unmitigated Deliberately not solved here - this is the bet
Infra risk Hosted on free GitHub Pages, no custom domain, depends on me maintaining the repo Accepted Low cost at this stage, revisit if this becomes long-term rather than job-search tooling
Fragility Static file means CV download breaks if filenames don't match exactly Resolved Already hit this bug in production. Fixed. Lesson learned the embarrassing way.

9. Open Questions / Future Work

  • No analytics instrumented - I don't actually know if anyone reads past the hero, or which case study lands hardest.
  • No CMS - fine at current scale, would need a lighter editing workflow if this grows.
  • The "In Progress" skill tier (Strategic Product Thinking) is the only forward-looking claim on the page. Worth revisiting whether one honest gap is the right amount of vulnerability to signal, or whether to add a second.
  • Untested: whether the conservative fallback variant actually gets used in practice, or whether I default to the bold version regardless of employer type.
  • Whether building a secret PRD page about a portfolio site was a good use of an evening or a sign I should be doing literally anything else. Pending further research.

If you found this through the console message: hi, you're exactly the kind of person I want reading this. If you found it through the footer: also hi, same energy, slightly different method.
