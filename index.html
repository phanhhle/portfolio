#ONH-007 assignee: mepriority: high ✓ Resolved ↗ onehub.vn
Marketplace Trust
Getting agents who don't trust each other to close deals together
OneHub, One Mount · Product Manager · Dec 2025 – Present
Picture this: a brand-new platform asking real estate agents to collaborate with total strangers — agent A has the listing, agent B has the buyer, and somehow they're supposed to team up and actually close the deal together. In a market where everyone's instinct is to guard their own client. No pressure.

So instead of designing the "nice" version of collaboration nobody would actually use, I went and sat with agents to map how deals truly move — the messy, territorial, real version. Several design and engineering squads turned that mess into actual architecture.

The platform now runs multiple agent workflows side by side, with zero forced "one true way" to collaborate — because two agents who've never met still need a way to split a deal and both walk away happy.

#HRD-014 assignee: mepriority: high ✓ Resolved ↗ herond.org/wallet
Web3 Onboarding
Why curious users never made it past step one
Herond Wallet, Herond Labs · Product Manager · Sept 2024 – Jul 2025
Herond Wallet was elbowing for space against giants like MetaMask — fighting over people who already spoke fluent Web3. Exhausting, crowded, and frankly a little beside the point.

The real opportunity was sitting right there, ignored: people genuinely curious about crypto who'd never touched a wallet in their life. Interviews across multiple user segments kept circling back to one specific villain: the seed phrase screen, where curious people quietly noped out. I led the Keyless Wallet initiative to remove it — a hard internal sell, since literally every major wallet still does it the old way.

Setup got noticeably faster, and it reshaped the whole pitch: not "Web3 made simple," but Web3 made for people who'd never need to know what a seed phrase even is.

#NBN-022 assignee: mepriority: medium ✓ Resolved ↗ nobinobi.vn
E-commerce Friction
Finding the friction hiding in plain sight
Nobinobi, Gianty Vietnam · Product Owner · Feb 2024 – Jul 2024
Engagement on Nobinobi had quietly flatlined, and everyone had a theory. Loud colors! Bad copy! Mercury in retrograde! None of them were backed by anything except vibes.

So I benched the opinions and went straight to buyer and seller behavior data until the actual friction points stopped hiding behind the noise. Once they were specific enough to act on, design and engineering shipped targeted fixes — not a redesign, just surgery on the parts that were genuinely broken.

More people who started a task actually finished it. Turns out users don't need a prettier app — they need the three extra clicks removed.
